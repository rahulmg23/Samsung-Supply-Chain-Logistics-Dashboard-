## Executive Summary

Samsung's Supply Chain & Logistics Dashboard is a **6-page enterprise-grade Power BI solution** covering the full value chain — from raw procurement through supplier management, manufacturing, inventory, shipment execution, and final sales performance.

At a glance, the business is generating **$4.50bn in total revenue** at a **22.5% profit margin**, fulfilling **152M units** in order volume — but operational inefficiencies are visible: only **50.37% on-time delivery**, a **33%+ shipment delay rate**, and an **inventory position of 9,000+ days of stock coverage** signal serious working capital and logistics risks demanding immediate leadership attention.

#### Inventory KPIs

| Metric | Value | Signal |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Inventory Turnover | **4.03%** |  Near-zero turnover — stock not moving relative to value |
| Days of Inventory | **9,070** |  ~25 years of stock coverage — data anomaly or serious overstock |
| Stock Coverage Days | **9,070** | Consistent with above |
| Inventory Accuracy | **—** | Not populated — data gap |
| Inventory Value | **$90.16bn** | Total book value of held inventory |

>  **Analyst Flag:** Days of Inventory at 9,070 is almost certainly a data modelling issue (possibly daily vs. annual normalization mismatch). However, if directionally correct, this represents catastrophic working capital lock-up and must be investigated immediately.

#### Key Visuals

**Inventory Value by Product:**
Family Hub Refrigerator ($12bn) and Galaxy Z Fold5 ($11bn) are the two highest-value inventory positions. QLED 85" 4K ($11bn) and OLED 65" 4K ($9bn) round out the top tier. Premium/large-format products dominate inventory value.

**Current Stock Level by Product:**
Galaxy S24 Ultra leads at 10M units, followed by Galaxy Z Fold5 (8.7M). These two products alone represent significant capital tied up in flagship inventory.

**Understock vs. Overstock Count by Product:**
Critically, **overstock dominates across nearly all SKUs** — with Galaxy S24 Ultra showing 396 overstock instances vs. 202 understock. Galaxy Z Fold5 shows 362 overstock vs. 200 understock. This suggests systematic over-procurement or demand forecasting failures on flagship lines.

**Stock Level vs. Safety Stock vs. Reorder Point:**
Current stock levels are **significantly above safety stock thresholds** across all products — confirming the systemic overstock condition. Reorder points are clustered near zero relative to actual holdings, meaning automatic replenishment triggers are largely irrelevant given current excess.
